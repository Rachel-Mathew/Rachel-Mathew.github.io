About Kerala

Kerala, a state on India's tropical Malabar Coast, has nearly 600km of Arabian Sea shoreline. It's known for its palm-lined beaches and backwaters, a network of canals. Inland are the Western Ghats, mountains whose slopes support tea, coffee and spice plantations as well as wildlife.

District Descriptions

Thiruvananthapuram

Thiruvananthapuram is the capital city of Kerala. It is known for its pristine beaches, historic landmarks, and rich cultural heritage.

Kollam

Kollam is famous for its backwaters, cashew industry, and historic trade connections. It is one of the oldest ports in the Arabian Sea.

Pathanamthitta

Pathanamthitta is known for its pilgrimage destinations, including the famous Sabarimala temple. The district is also rich in natural beauty with numerous rivers and forests.

Alappuzha

Alappuzha, also known as Alleppey, is renowned for its picturesque canals, backwaters, beaches, and houseboat cruises. It is often referred to as the 'Venice of the East'.

Kottayam

Kottayam is known for its literacy and educational achievements. It is also famous for its picturesque landscapes and rubber plantations.

Idukki

Idukki is a landlocked district known for its rugged terrain, dense forests, and wildlife sanctuaries. It is home to the Idukki Arch Dam, one of the highest arch dams in Asia.

Ernakulam

Ernakulam is the commercial capital of Kerala and is known for its cosmopolitan culture. The city of Kochi in Ernakulam is a major port city with a vibrant mix of old and new.

Thrissur

Thrissur is known as the cultural capital of Kerala. It hosts the famous Thrissur Pooram festival and is also known for its ancient temples and churches.

Palakkad

Palakkad is known for its extensive paddy fields, mountains, and wildlife sanctuaries. It is also famous for the Palakkad Fort, which dates back to the 18th century.

Malappuram

Malappuram is known for its rich cultural heritage and history. It is also a center for Islamic learning and culture in Kerala.

Kozhikode

Kozhikode, also known as Calicut, is known for its historic significance as a major trading port. It is also famous for its culinary delights and serene beaches.

Wayanad

Wayanad is known for its scenic beauty, waterfalls, wildlife, and tribal culture. It is a popular tourist destination for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts.

Kannur

Kannur is known for its handloom industry, folklore, and cultural heritage. It is also home to several beautiful beaches and historical forts.

Kasaragod

Kasaragod is known for its coir and handloom industries. It is also famous for the Bekal Fort, which is one of the largest and best-preserved forts in Kerala.
